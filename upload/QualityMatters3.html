Underlying Principles

Over time, Quality Matters has become a self-supporting, non-profit organization and uses the following underlying principles to guide its work:

Continuous

  • The process is designed to ensure that all reviewed courses will eventually meet expectations.
  • The rubric-based review is integral to a continuous quality improvement process.

Centered

  • On research: The development of the rubric is based on national standards of best practice, the research literature, and instructional design principles.

Collegial

  • A Quality Matters review is part of a faculty-driven, peer review process.
  • The review process is intended to be diagnostic and collegial, not evaluative and judgmental.

Collaborative

  • The review is based on collaboratively identified evidence found in the course rather than the personal preference of an individual reviewer.
  • The review is flexible and not prescriptive (many ways to meet each standard).
  • The review team consists of three experienced online instructors as reviewers along with the course faculty developer.
